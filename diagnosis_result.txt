**AGRI-SCIENTIST REPORT**

**1. DISEASE IDENTIFICATION**
*   **Common Name:** Early Blight
*   **Scientific Name:** *Alternaria solani*

**2. SYMPTOMS**
*   **Target-like Concentric Rings:** The most distinct feature visible in the photo is the large, dark brown necrotic lesion showing characteristic "bull’s eye" concentric rings. This is a classic diagnostic sign of *Alternaria* infection.
*   **Chlorotic Halo:** Each dark brown spot is surrounded by a prominent yellow (chlorotic) halo, indicating that the fungus is releasing toxins that destroy the chlorophyll in the surrounding healthy tissue.
*   **Irregular Necrotic Spots:** Besides the primary large lesion, smaller, irregular brown spots are beginning to develop across the leaflet, which will eventually merge and cause the entire leaf to wither and drop (defoliation).

**3. CONFIDENCE LEVEL**
*   **98% Confidence:** The symptoms are textbook examples of Early Blight in Solanaceous crops.

**4. IMMEDIATE TREATMENT (Chemical)**
To stop the spread of spores, immediate application of a contact or systemic fungicide is required:
*   **Mancozeb (e.g., Indofil M-45):** Mix 2.5 grams per 1 liter of water and spray thoroughly on both sides of the leaves.
*   **Chlorothalonil (e.g., Kavach):** Mix 2 grams per 1 liter of water. This acts as a protective barrier against further fungal penetration.
*   *Note: Repeat the spray every 10–14 days if the weather remains humid.*

**5. ORGANIC TREATMENT**
*   **Neem Oil:** Use a high-quality Neem oil (1500 ppm or higher). Mix 5ml of Neem oil and 1ml of liquid soap in 1 liter of water. Spray every 7 days.
*   **Trichoderma viride:** Apply *Trichoderma viride* formulation (10g per liter) as a foliar spray and also enrich the soil around the base to suppress soil-borne fungal inoculum.

**6. PREVENTION**
*   **Crop Rotation:** Do not plant tomatoes, potatoes, or peppers in the same patch of land for at least 3 years, as the fungus survives in the soil.
*   **Pruning:** Remove the lower leaves (bottom 12 inches) of the plant to prevent soil-borne spores from splashing onto the foliage during rain or irrigation.
*   **Mulching:** Use plastic or straw mulch to create a barrier between the soil and the plant leaves.
*   **Water Management:** Always water at the base of the plant (drip irrigation). Avoid overhead sprinklers that keep the foliage wet, as moisture is essential for spore germination.
