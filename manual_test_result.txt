**SCIENTIFIC CROP HEALTH REPORT**

### 1. IDENTIFICATION
*   **Common Name:** Sorghum Downy Mildew
*   **Scientific Name:** *Peronosclerospora sorghi*

### 2. THE CAUSE (WHY IT HAPPENED)
This disease is caused by an oomycete (fungus-like organism). It is primarily **soil-borne**, where dormant spores called oospores can survive for up to 5-10 years. The infection in your field likely triggered due to **high soil moisture** during the early seedling stage followed by **high humidity (above 90%)** and moderate temperatures (21°C to 25°C). It is a systemic disease, meaning once it enters the roots or young leaves, it spreads throughout the entire plant's vascular system.

### 3. THE SPREAD (LIFECYCLE)
The disease has two phases of spread:
*   **Primary Spread:** Soil-borne oospores infect the roots of young seedlings.
*   **Secondary Spread:** During humid nights, the plant produces white "conidia" (spores) on the leaf surface, which are carried by **wind** to neighboring healthy plants, causing rapid field-wide infection.

### 4. KEY SYMPTOMS (Visible in your photo)
1.  **Chlorotic Striping:** Notice the distinct, pale-yellow to whitish longitudinal stripes running parallel to the leaf veins, starting from the base.
2.  **Stunting:** The affected plant shows restricted growth compared to healthy ones, with leaves appearing more narrow and erect.
3.  **Systemic Yellowing:** The upper leaves are showing more intense yellowing (chlorosis), indicating the pathogen has reached the growing point.

### 5. TREATMENT (IMMEDIATE)
Since this is a systemic infection, you must act quickly to save the surrounding crop:
*   **Rogueing:** Immediately pull out and **burn** the severely infected plants (like the one in the photo) to stop the production of airborne spores.
*   **Chemical Spray 1:** Spray **Metalaxyl 8% + Mancozeb 64% WP** (commercially known as Ridomil Gold) at a dosage of **2.5 grams per liter** of water.
*   **Chemical Spray 2:** Alternatively, use **Azoxystrobin 18.2% + Cyproconazole 7.3% SC** at a dosage of **1 ml per liter** of water. Ensure thorough coverage of both sides of the leaves.

### 6. ORGANIC CURE
*   **Soil Treatment:** Apply *Trichoderma viride* or *Pseudomonas fluorescens* enriched in well-rotted Farm Yard Manure (FYM) around the base of the plants (5kg per acre mixed with 50kg FYM).
*   **Foliar Spray:** Use a **3% Neem Oil solution** (30ml per liter of water with a little soap) to boost the plant's systemic resistance and reduce secondary spore germination.

### 7. LONG-TERM PREVENTION
1.  **Seed Treatment:** In the next season, always treat seeds with **Metalaxyl 35% WS @ 6g/kg** of seed. This is the most effective way to prevent Downy Mildew.
2.  **Crop Rotation:** Do not plant maize or sorghum in this specific field for at least 2 years. Rotate with non-grass crops like pulses or oilseeds to break the pathogen's lifecycle.
3.  **Deep Summer Plowing:** After harvest, perform deep plowing to expose the soil-borne oospores to extreme solar heat, which kills them naturally.

Be proactive, Satyam. Removing the infected plants now is the most important step to save the rest of your field.
